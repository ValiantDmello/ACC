GPS and car audio Hate asking for directions? No problem. TomTom and Garmin offer a huge selection of GPS systems that will get you from A to B and back again without ever stopping to ask if you’re headed in the right direction. If you’re looking to create the ultimate audio setup in your vehicle, you’ll need the right car electronics. A car deck, subwoofer, amp, and speakers are all designed to work flawlessly together to give you the most incredible sound that can rattle your windows or the neighbourhood. Choose a head unit that support Apple CarPlay or Android Auto to fully integrate your smartphone for the ultimate hands-free, connected driving experience. Car electronics add new features Get a car starter and/or alarm system professionally installed. A starter will ensure that you’re getting into a warm car every time during those cold winter months, while an alarm system will help keep your car electronics safe from theft. Install a dashcam so you can automatically record everything going on in the road ahead of you—valuable data to have in the event of an accident. Best Buy also carries a selection of SiriusXM satellite radios and accessories so you can enjoy tons of talk, music, sports, and entertainment programs in the car or in your home. And if you spend any time on the water, you’ll want to hook yourself up with some marine electronics too. Backup cams and rear-view cams Many new cars and trucks come with backup cameras, and with good reason. Safety is greatly improved when you can clearly see what’s behind your vehicle, even if you don’t have a great view using your mirrors. Just because your car lacks the feature doesn’t mean you can’t easily add it. Backup cams and rear-view cams mount in your back window or even on your license plate, with their output displayed on the display of a compatible car stereo system, a supplied LCD display, or your smartphone. Wired and wireless (battery-powered) options are available. Car gadgets with built-in voice assistant You don’t have to leave Siri or Google Assistant at home if you upgrade your car audio system to one that includes Apple CarPlay or Android Auto support. Your smartphone connects to the stereo using Bluetooth (or through a wired connection if you also want to keep the battery charged), so you can play streaming audio. Use your favourite digital assistant to safely make calls, reply to messages and give driving directions.

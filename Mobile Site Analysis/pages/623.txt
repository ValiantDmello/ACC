Processors The processor is the brain of the laptop, interpreting and performing all its commands. Intel and AMD both make processors that are tailor-made for different needs—from basic tasks like sending emails to demanding ones like simultaneously running multiple applications. To ensure you’ve got the right processor for your needs, consider individual specs like number of cores, overclock ability and speed. Screen Size and Resolution Size matters when it comes to laptops. A larger screen will likely make the device heavier but will give you plenty of room to work and play. A smaller screen size will make it lighter and more compact, perfect for taking on the go. Most laptops have a screen resolution of at least Full HD (1920 x 1080), and there are many options even higher resolution, even 4K! Family Friendly Laptops Many people are looking for a versatile and dependable machine that is great for the whole family. There are many great options available that offer great processing power, amazing screen resolution and responsiveness and are designed to handle a wide variety of purposes. For example, Microsoft Surface family includes the tablet style Surface Pro, the Surface Laptop and the Surface Book—there is machine to suit anyone’s needs. For people more familiar with Apple computers, there are a variety of configurations, including the MacBook, MacBook Pro and MacBook Air, to ensure no matter how demanding the job, you’ll be able to complete it on your MacBook. Your children will excel with any of these computer: Microsoft and Apple manufacture some of the best laptops for students. 2 in 1 and Hybrid Laptops A 2 in 1 laptop (also called convertible or hybrid laptop) combines the functionality of a laptop with the convenience of a tablet. Equipped with both a touchscreen and a keyboard, a 2 in 1 laptop can transform from laptop to tablet in various ways—by folding back, rotating or detaching the screen, depending on the model. A 2 in 1 is perfect when you want both a laptop and a tablet without having to buy two separate devices.
